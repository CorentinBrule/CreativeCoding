    Obituaries

    Highlights

    1. David Seeley, Educator Who Fought Against Segregation, Dies at 85

      Dr. Seeley worked to provide equal opportunity to young people, through political actions and community alliances, for more than 50 years.

      By SAM ROBERTS

    2. E. Lee Hennessee, a Leading Woman in Hedge Funds, Dies at 64

      Ms. Hennessee created one of the first indexes to track the hedge fund industry’s secretive transactions.

      By JULIE CRESWELL

      1. Richard Steiner, Producer of Broadway Hits, Dies at 69

        Mr. Steiner worked a broad variety of jobs and was, among other things, a poker champion.

        By WILLIAM GRIMES

      2. Gene La Rocque, Decorated Veteran Who Condemned Waste of War, Dies at 98

        After retiring from the Navy, Admiral La Rocque founded a think tank to promote peace and to monitor the influence of the military-industrial complex.

        By ANITA GATES

    1. Kay Starr, Hillbilly Singer With Crossover Appeal, Dies at 94

      Ms. Starr found fame in the big-band era, hit it big as a pop and country artist, and continued her career well into her later years.

      By DAVID BELCHER

    2. Miriam Weinstein, Mother and Backbone of Original Miramax, Dies at 90

      Ms. Weinstein had no plans to stop contributing (advice or pastries), even after Miramax grew in fame and value.

      By ANITA GATES

    3. Robert Weber Dies at 92; His New Yorker Cartoons Twitted the Overprivileged

      The people Mr. Weber drew over 45 years for the magazine were nothing if not entitled, going about with what some might see as skewed expectations.

      By ANITA GATES

    4. Stanford Lipsey, 89, Publisher Whose Nebraska Weeklies Won Pulitzer, Dies

      Mr. Lipsey’s Omaha chain, the Sun Newspapers, won in 1973 for exposing fund-raising excesses by Boys Town, the charity for orphaned children.

      By SAM ROBERTS

    5. Notable Deaths 2016

      Remembering Nancy Reagan, David Bowie, Harper Lee, Merle Haggard, Anita Brookner, Clyde Lovellette, Anne Jackson, Monte Irvin, Maurice White, Umberto Eco and others who died this year.

      By THE NEW YORK TIMES

    Latest Articles

    1. Don Marshall, Who Made Casting History in ‘Land of the Giants,’ Is Dead at 80

      Mr. Marshall, one of the first black actors to have a starring role on an American network television series, also appeared in television movies and feature films.

      By ANITA GATES
      Nov. 2, 2016

    2. Start-Ups for the End of Life

      A crop of tech start-ups is looking to capture a slice of the funeral sector and end-of-life care. Many of them are founded by millennials, who have grown up online.

      By EILENE ZIMMERMAN
      Nov. 2, 2016

    3. John Hicks, Star Lineman at Ohio State and Top Rookie for the Giants, Dies at 65

      An Outland Trophy winner under Woody Hayes with the Buckeyes, he played for the Giants in the N.F.L. after being drafted third over all.

      By DANIEL E. SLOTNIK
      Nov. 1, 2016

    4. Natalie Babbitt, 84, Dies; Took On Immortality in ‘Tuck Everlasting’

      Ms. Babbitt’s book (one of many) about a family that cannot die found a fervent readership and inspired two films and a Broadway musical.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      Nov. 1, 2016

    5. David H. Brooks, 61, Dies Serving Time for Insider Trading

      The founder of the military armor maker DHB Industries was serving 17 years for insider trading and fraudulently enriching himself.

      By REED ABELSON
      Nov. 1, 2016

    6. Curly Putman, Writer of ‘The Green, Green Grass of Home,’ Dies at 85

      Mr. Putman turned out hundreds of songs, many of them country chart-toppers, after moving to Nashville and signing with Tree Publishing in the early 1960s.

      By WILLIAM GRIMES
      Oct. 31, 2016

    7. Norman Brokaw, Agent to Marilyn Monroe and Elvis Presley, Dies at 89

      Mr. Brokaw rose from teenage mail clerk to top mogul at the William Morris talent agency, where he broke ground in the fledgling television industry.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      Oct. 31, 2016

    8. Tammy Grimes, the Original ‘Unsinkable Molly Brown,’ Dies at 82

      The throaty actress and singer won a Tony Award for her performance in “The Unsinkable Molly Brown” at age 26 and went on to a distinguished stage career.

      By ANITA GATES
      Oct. 31, 2016

    9. James Galanos, Fashion Designer for the Elite, Dies at 92

      Mr. Galanos, who outfitted Nancy Reagan for inaugural events, earned numerous accolades even as he evaded celebrity and shunned the fashion establishment.

      By GUY TREBAY
      Oct. 31, 2016

    10. W. Dudley Johnson, Heart Bypass Surgery Pioneer, Dies at 86

      Dr. Johnson, who applied crocheting and needlecraft skills, went on to perform more than 8,500 bypass operations over four decades.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      Oct. 30, 2016

    2. Pen Sovann, Cambodia’s First Prime Minister After Khmer Rouge, Dies at 80

      Mr. Pen Sovann was installed after Vietnam defeated the brutal Khmer Rouge regime, only to be ousted and imprisoned six months later.

      By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
      Oct. 30, 2016

    3. Richard Pittman, Marine Who Fended Off Vietnam Ambush, Dies at 71

      Mr. Pittman earned the Medal of Honor for charging into heavy North Vietnamese fire to aid wounded fellow Marines.

      By DANIEL E. SLOTNIK
      Oct. 29, 2016

    4. Susan Lindquist, Scientist Who Made Genetic Discoveries Using Yeast, Dies at 67

      The conceptually daring work of Dr. Lindquist, a National Medal of Science recipient, opened new paths to understanding Parkinson’s and other diseases.

      By WILLIAM GRIMES
      Oct. 28, 2016

    5. Sophia Cranshaw, MTV Producer of Social-Issue Campaigns, Dies at 45

      Ms. Cranshaw won awards for public service announcements and campaigns that dealt with everything from genocide in Darfur to depression on campus.

      By ANITA GATES
      Oct. 28, 2016

    6. John Zacherle, Host With a Ghoulish Perspective, Dies at 98

      Mr. Zacherle was one of early television’s horror-movie hosts, playing an undertaker and other characters on stations in Philadelphia and New York in the 1950s and ’60s.

      By WILLIAM GRIMES
      Oct. 28, 2016

    7. Michael Massee, 64, Screen Villain Haunted by the Accidental Death of Brandon Lee, Dies

      Mr. Massee, a character actor, took a long sabbatical after the deadly shooting on the set of “The Crow” in 1993 and then appeared in many TV and film roles.

      By DANIEL E. SLOTNIK
      Oct. 27, 2016

    8. Ewen Whitaker, Who Guided NASA to the Moon, Dies at 94

      Mr. Whitaker had no formal training as an astronomer, but he used his unparalleled knowledge of the lunar surface to select landing sites for spacecraft.

      By WILLIAM GRIMES
      Oct. 27, 2016

    9. Gavin MacFadyen, 76, Mentor and Defender of WikiLeaks Founder, Dies

      The American investigative journalist and documentarian was a core supporter of WikiLeaks and Julian Assange and provided support of whistle-blowers.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      Oct. 27, 2016

    10. Prince Mikasa, Brother of Emperor Hirohito of Japan, Dies at 100

      Prince Mikasa, an Imperial Army veteran who became a pacifist, was the uncle of the current emperor and fifth in line to the throne.

      By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
      Oct. 27, 2016

    11. Pete Burns, Frontman for the Band Dead or Alive, Dies at 57

      Mr. Burns gained fame for the 1985 hit “You Spin Me Round (Like a Record)” and later became known for his plastic surgery.

      By DANIEL E. SLOTNIK
      Oct. 27, 2016
