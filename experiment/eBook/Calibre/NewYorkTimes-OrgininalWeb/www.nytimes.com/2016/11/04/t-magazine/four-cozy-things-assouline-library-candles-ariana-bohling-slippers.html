    Four Cozy Things T Editors Are Really Into Right Now

    NOV. 4, 2016

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    Warm, Furry Slippers

    If Ariana Bohling’s alpaca slippers look insanely soft, it’s because they are. Available exclusively at NoLita’s eco-friendly design boutique Côte à Coast, they are made in Peru, where alpacas are a protected species, from animals that die naturally while roaming freely in their native habitat. The slippers are beyond cozy, not to mention the bonus entertainment factor: They make your feet look like Dr. Seuss characters. $175, available in men’s and women’s sizes, 350 Bowery, New York, NY. — MARY KAYE SCHILLING

    Candles to Turn Any Room Into a Library

    If heaven exists, mine is a library in the woods upstate, complete with those sliding bookcase ladders and perpetual magic-hour light streaming through big bay windows. My eighth-floor Battery Park City studio apartment is … not that. So I’m very grateful for Assouline’s new collection of Library candles, with which I can be momentarily transported. In five bookish scents — Leather, Paper, Wood, the cedar-and-patchouli-spiked Havana and a fresh vanilla-sandalwood option called Culture Lounge — the set, or any single one, would also make a good holiday gift for the man (or woman!) who always stumps you. $50 each, assouline.com. — ALEXANDRIA SYMONDS

    A Robe To Live In

    The new men’s version of Hill House Home’s navy silk charmeuse robe with thin white piping was named after Truman Capote — because of his penchant for working from bed. Monogrammable (and, come to think of it, a great holiday gift), it’s a thing that could be equally at home in 1956 or 2016. $299, hillhousehome.com. — ISABEL WILKINSON

    Very Thick Blankets

    Though the interiors stylist and designer Kim Ficaro is based in New York, her new cotton blankets are handmade, like most fashionable textiles, in Morocco. And though billed as medium weight, the monochromatic designs are heavy enough to add serious warmth to a bed (a queen, specifically) or sofa this winter. They are thicker than they are soft — and with oversize pompoms dancing from the shorter edges, as delightful as they are minimal. $275, available in gray, white, teal and striped, totemhome.com. — ALAINNA LEXIE BEDDIE
